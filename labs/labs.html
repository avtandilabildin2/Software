Computer Labs

The department maintains several instructional computing laboratories in B-Block, open to students. Labs can be reserved for instructional purposes and are open for walk-in use when not in use.

The Electronic Lab (Lab-1)

Operated by the Department of Industrial Electronics. Used for laboratory classes, research, and personal study.

Specifications: 1 computer, projector, marker board, soldering equipment, 0-24V Power supplies, oscillators, TTL & CMOS ICs, resistors (1Ω–2MΩ), capacitors (0.1–470 mF), transistors (BC547, 109, 2N2222, LM317), diodes, EMI tools.

Applied Mathematics Lab (Lab-2)

Operated by the Department of Applied Mathematics. Used for laboratory classes, research, and personal study.

Specifications: 21 computers, projector, marker board, 1 server + 20 clients, Windows 7, software: Matlab, Maple, Java, Delphi, Visual C, Python, etc.

Networking Lab (Lab-3)

Operated by the Department of Computer Engineering. Used for laboratory classes, research, and personal study.

Specifications: 25 computers, projector, marker board, 1 server + 24 clients, Windows 7, software: Eclipse, Delphi, VMware, Visual Studio, etc.

Software Development Lab (Lab-4)

Operated by the Department of Computer Engineering. Used for laboratory classes, research, and personal study.

Specifications: 25 computers, projector, marker board, Linux Fedora OS, precompiled software.

Electronics Lab (Lab-6)

Operated by the Department of Industrial Electronics. Supports advanced experiments in signals, communications, electromagnetism, and photonics.

Specifications: 9 computers, projector, marker board, 31 stations with Lenovo ThinkCentre, Agilent multimeters, power supplies, function generators, oscilloscopes (HP/Agilent MSO6012A & LeCroy WaveSurfer 422), all devices connected to computers.

Smart Class

Operated by the Department of Computer Science. Used for distance learning, online seminars, collaboration courses, and teleconferences.

Specifications: 1 laptop, 1 smart board, short-distance projector, LED TV, Web IP Camera, microphones, and sound system.

Lab Policies and Conditions of Use

  1. General Computer Lab Policy applies. Refer to link.
  2. Network Lab is available only to staff, students of Faculty of New Technologies, and official visitors.
  3. Others require permission from Head of Computer Science Department.
